THEOREM 1: Supposed f and g be two real functions continuous at a real no. c.Then
(1)f+g is continuous at x=c.
(2)f-g is continuous at x=c.
(3)f.g is continuous at x=c.
(4)f/g is continuous at x=c, (provided g(c) not equal to 0)

Proof: We are investigating continuity of $(f+g)$ at $x=c$.Clearly it is defined at $x=c$.We have \begin{eqnarray*} \lim_{x \rightarrow c}(f+g)(x) & = & \lim_{x \rightarrow c}[f(x)+(x)] \\ & = & \lim_{x \rightarrow c}f(x) + \lim_{x \rightarrow c}g(x) \\ & = & f(c) + g(c) \\ & = & (f+g)(c) \end{eqnarray*}

Hence, f+g is continuous at x=c

Proofs for the remaining parts are similar
