Example:Show that the function defined by
f(x)=|1-x+|x||

,where x is any real number, is a continuous function.

Solution:Define g by g(x)=1-x+|x| and h by h(x)=|x| for all real x.Then

\begin{eqnarray*} (hog)(x) & = & h(g(x))\\ & = & h(1-x+|x|)\\ & = & |1-x+|x||\\ & = & f(x)\\ \end{eqnarray*}

We already know that h is a continuous function.Hence g being a sum of a polynomial function and the modulus function is continuous.But then f being a composite of two continuous functions is continuous.
